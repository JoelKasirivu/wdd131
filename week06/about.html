Our Mission

We believe in the power of compassion and community. Our programs focus on education, health, and sustainable development.

In His Hands received 501(c)(3) non-profit status in 2008, as a result of the calling Michelle Delhaute McGowan had to change the lives of orphans worldwide. Since that time, In His Hands has provided homes, loving care, food, education, medicine, and life-saving surgeries for children around the world. Orphan awareness, orphan prevention, holistic care for orphans, and adoption support are the causes that drive us forward. Our work spans many countries, but our focus is on Haiti, Kenya, Myanmar, the Philippines and the United States.

In 2015, In His Hands purchased 6.375 acres in Grande Savanne, Haiti and thanks to donors support, a mission house, 6 orphan homes, bathhouses, an infirmary, guest house, kitchen, pavilion, intern triplex, and guard station were built. Orphans are lovingly cared for in the homes by house parents and the children attend the local school and church. Your funds provide food, clothing, education, tutoring, medical care, Haitian staff salaries and more. In His Hands is also involved in the community and provides small business loans to needy people to help them better provide for their families. After the 2021 earthquake, your relief support provided food, clean water and clothing to affected victims. Typically, around 40 children are in our care at any given time. After the 2023 flood, you provided hundreds of meals to flood victims.

Kenya is our newest program, which we began funding in April 2022. We work with pastors to provide food, education and medical care for orphans, and feed 92 people a day. In addition, we work with widows who are caring for orphans in their homes and help them to start small businesses so they can better support themselves and the children in their care. In His Hands purchased 2 acres of land in Kenya and this is the future site of a fresh water well that will provide clean water for the community, a home for orphans, a kitchen, pavilion, and medical center.
